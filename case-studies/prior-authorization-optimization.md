# Prior Authorization Optimization
**AI + Workflow Design + Operational Optimization**

## Problem
Prior authorization workflows are often complex, time-sensitive, and heavily dependent on manual processes. Delays in documentation review, inconsistent requirements across payers, and fragmented communication between providers and internal teams can slow approvals and increase administrative burden.

## Current Workflow
- Providers submit prior authorization requests with supporting documentation  
- Requests are reviewed manually for completeness and accuracy  
- Additional information is often required, leading to back-and-forth communication  
- Teams navigate multiple systems to verify benefits, requirements, and status  
- High volumes and variability contribute to delays in decision-making

## Pain Points
- Delays caused by incomplete or inconsistent documentation  
- Repetitive manual review processes  
- Lack of standardization across payers  
- High administrative workload for staff  
- Limited visibility into request status and bottlenecks

## Proposed AI Solution
- AI-assisted documentation review to identify missing or incomplete information early  
- Intelligent prompts to guide providers toward correct submission requirements  
- Automated status tracking and updates across systems  
- Decision support tools to assist with authorization criteria validation  
- Prioritization of requests based on urgency and clinical impact

## Expected Impact
- Faster authorization turnaround times  
- Reduced back-and-forth communication  
- Improved accuracy and completeness of submissions  
- Lower administrative burden on operational teams  
- Enhanced visibility into workflow status and performance

## Skills Demonstrated
- Healthcare operations analysis  
- Workflow optimization  
- AI solution design  
- Process improvement in high-volume environments  
- Systems thinking and cross-functional awareness

## Notes
This case study is based on real-world healthcare experience and reflects common operational challenges, without including proprietary or sensitive data.
